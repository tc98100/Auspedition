GUIDE TO UNIVERSITY OF SYDNEY

Discover Some Sunshine And Good Times In University Of Sydney

By Bonnie Wu

The University of Sydney was founded in 1850 and is located in Sydney, Australia's largest city, making it the oldest university in the country. It's also ranked in the top 10 most beautiful campuses in the world! At the same time, the University of Sydney is one of the p remier halls of academia in the entire southern hemisphere and a globally renowned institution of higher learning. There are many prominent politicians and business people who study here. An interesting rumor to share with you is that many people come here not because of the academic status of the University of Sydney, but because they don't know who said that the Harry Potter movie was set here. The fact is that Harry Potter was not actually set here, but it's just like Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, a very old and mysterious building.

As a student here for 3 years, let me share with you some of my favorite and frequently visited campus attractions.

  • The Quadrangle
  • Graffiti Tunnel

Getting to campus

The Quadrangle

The design of the main Fontenoy building follows the tradition of British universities. The glass paintings in the Great Hall include windows from Oxford, Cambridge and the Royal Family. The stonework also features the heads of Queen Victoria and the Prince, the initials "VR" and the royal motto. You'll also find royal symbols in the main Fontenoy building: the crown and the Tudor rose. The Great Hall is used for different events such as graduation ceremonies. In the right hand corner of the main building is a beautiful Jacaranda tree, and it's said that when it's in full bloom, it's too late to study. However, for us students, reading under the blue tree and listening to the melodious music of the zither was a very pleasant experience. Also, the Nicholson Museum is highly recommended to visit. The collection includes valuable historical artefacts from Ancient Greece, Italy and the Mediterranean civilisation, and is the largest European antiques museum in Australia.

Graffiti Tunnel

The graffiti tunnel is a legal graffiti wall located between the Holme Building and Manning House, and it is the only place on campus where spray paint is allowed. It is the only place on campus where graffiti can be spray painted, but university authorities and campus security have ruled that only "civilized" works of art are allowed, and no graffiti can be sprayed on doorknobs. We share our insights and ideas on this street and feel the different cultures we pass on. It is also a popular place for students and tourists to take photos.
